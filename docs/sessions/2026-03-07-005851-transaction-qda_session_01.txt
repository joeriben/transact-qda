  Key for Our Project

  The productive question you raised — whether D/B's naming forms help us identify Prozessierungsformen for our data objects — I think the answer is:
  partially yes, but with a crucial extension.

  What D/B give us is a processual gradient of determination: Cue → Characterization → Specification. This maps well onto a research process: something
  first appears as a vague signal (cue), gets provisionally named (characterization), and is progressively specified through inquiry. Every naming in our
  system could carry a marker of where it sits on this gradient.

  But — and this connects to your point about destructive interference — D/B's gradient is monotonically toward more determination. It doesn't account for
  the reverse movement: the deliberate de-specification, the moment when something well-determined dissolves back into superposition, or when a silence is
  registered. The Barad-point: that the zero of interference is itself a positive finding.

  So I'd propose: D/B's Cue → Characterization → Specification gives us one axis of processuality. But we need a second axis that D/B don't provide —
  something like resolution/dissolution or collapse/superposition — to capture the symmetry you described between presence and absence, between the visible
  and the concretely-not-there.

  Shall we sketch how this maps onto the data model concretely? For instance, should appearances carry a field indicating their designational stage
  (cue/characterization/specification)?
